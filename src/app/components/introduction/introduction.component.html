Guilty Crown (Anime)

In 2029, with a sudden encounter and spread of the epidemic "Apocalypse Virus", Japan fell into chaos and became a state of anarchy. In response, "GHQ" was organized under supranational military intervention and placed under its rule; with Japan losing its body of an independent state. This incident is later known as "Lost Christmas". Given the country's autonomy, the people enjoyed transient peace. As time flows to the next ten years of 2039, a second year high school student by the name of Ouma Shu, attends Tennoz High and lives a peaceful day-to-day life. However, that peace is broken one day when he encounters Yuzuriha Inori, a famous web vocalist, who also on the other hand, doubles surreptitiously as a member of "Funeral Parlor"; a resistance group led by a young Tsutsugami Gai, aimed to liberate Japan from the rule of the "GHQ". As Shu gets embroiled into the group's activities, the "Mark of The King" appears on his right hand, earning him the ability to freely draw out a person's "Void"(a physical metaphor) and use it as a weapon of convenience. However, that also becomes the "Crown of Sin" that Shu has to shoulder.

Guilty Crown: Lost Christmas

Guilty Crown: Lost Christmas is a game that was released from the new series Guilty Crown on 26th July 2012 for the PC. It is based around the time of the "Lost Christmas" incident and was sold for 5040 yen for normal Edition. The Limited Edition with CG, Accessories and Manga was sold for 7140 yen.
